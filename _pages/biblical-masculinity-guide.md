The modern church has produced two equally inadequate models of Christian manhood. On one side: the sensitive, conflict-averse, perpetually apologetic "Christian nice guy" — passive in the home, passive at work, passive in the world. On the other side: an aggressive, posturing pseudo-masculinity that mistakes volume for authority and dominance for leadership.

Neither is what Scripture describes.

This guide is for the man who is done with both extremes and wants to understand what the Bible actually teaches about masculine identity, discipline, leadership, and warfare — and how to live it in the real conditions of modern life.

## The Foundation

Biblical masculinity is rooted in two creation texts and one New Testament arc. Genesis 1:26-28 commissions humanity (male and female) with dominion. Genesis 2:15 specifies the masculine mandate: *abad* (cultivate, serve) and *shamar* (guard, protect). The New Testament builds on this with the model of Christ as servant-king (Ephesians 5:25, Philippians 2:5-11) — authority used entirely in the interest of those served.
