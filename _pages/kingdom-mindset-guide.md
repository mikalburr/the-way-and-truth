## What "Kingdom Mindset" Actually Means

**Kingdom mindset** is the lived orientation of a Christian whose primary identity, allegiance, and operational framework comes from the Kingdom of God that Jesus announced — present, real, and active right now — rather than from the cultural, religious, or self-generated frameworks most men actually live by.

It is not positive thinking. It is not prosperity theology. It is not a mood or a feeling. It is the disciplined renewal of how you understand reality, yourself, your work, your relationships, and your future based on what Jesus actually said about the Kingdom he came to inaugurate.

## The Theological Foundation

The Kingdom of God is the central category of Jesus's preaching. Mark 1:15 records his first recorded words: *"The time has come. The Kingdom of God has come near. Repent and believe the good news."*

This was not a religious announcement. It was a political claim. Jesus was announcing the arrival of God's active reign in human history — already inaugurated, not yet fully consummated.

## The Identity Question — Who You Are in Christ

The Kingdom does not change what you do. It changes who you are — and who you are determines what you do.

Most men who have been in church for years still live out of an old identity that contradicts the new creation Paul describes (2 Corinthians 5:17). They were told they were "saved" but never told that the salvation included a complete re-identification: adopted son, joint heir, ambassador, citizen of heaven, seated with Christ in heavenly authority.
