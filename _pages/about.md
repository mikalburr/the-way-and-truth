I am a Marketing PhD candidate at Florida State University, a lifelong athlete, and a man who has staked his life on the truth of Jesus Christ. Not the institutional version. Not the watered-down, cultural Christianity that fills pews and produces nothing. The real thing — the Kingdom of God as Jesus actually described it, the radical identity he actually conferred, the demands he actually made.

This site exists because the internet is full of Christian content and nearly empty of Christian truth.

Most of what passes for Christian teaching online is either prosperity gospel dressed up in Scripture references, denominational politics masquerading as doctrine, or motivational content with a cross in the thumbnail. It is not malicious. It is just shallow. And shallow teaching produces shallow disciples.

The Way & Truth is built on one conviction: the words of Jesus Christ are sufficient. Not as a starting point. As the thing itself. The Sermon on the Mount is not an introduction to something more advanced. It is the advanced thing. Most people have never actually sat with it long enough to feel its weight.

My background is in marketing science — I study how people receive, process, and act on information. That lens shapes how I write here. I care about search intent because I want the person who types "what did Jesus actually teach" at 11pm on a Tuesday to find something real. Something that changes how they see their life. Not another listicle, not another devotional that asks nothing of them.

I also train. Discipline of the body matters to me not as vanity but as stewardship — the body is the temple, and a man who cannot govern his physical life rarely governs his spiritual one well either. That conviction runs through the Warrior Mindset content here.

Three pillars. One mission.

**Kingdom Mindset** — who you are in Christ and what the Kingdom of God actually means as a present reality.

**True Teachings** — what Jesus actually said, verse by verse, without tradition layered on top.

**Warrior Mindset** — what a man of God looks like when he takes the whole thing seriously.
