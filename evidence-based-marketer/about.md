## Why this site exists

Most marketing content trades on authority borrowed from culture, not earned from evidence. "10x growth hacks." Playbooks built on anecdotes. Frameworks with no falsifiable logic.

I started *The Evidence-Based Marketer* because the marketing education space deserves better — and because a PhD program taught me exactly what the gap is between what marketers *believe* and what the research *actually shows*.

This is not an academic journal. It's not a guru newsletter. It's the translation layer between peer-reviewed research and practical decision-making — written for the practitioner who wants to reason well, not just act fast.

## What I study (and why it matters here)

My dissertation sits at the intersection of **consumer psychology**, **quantitative research methods**, and **digital behavior**. That means everything I write here is informed by:

- Graduate-level training in experimental design and statistical analysis
- Deep reading in consumer behavior, persuasion, and decision science
- Real-world application across content, strategy, and entrepreneurship

If I cite a finding, I've read the study. If I recommend a method, I've used it.

## What you'll find here

**[Consumer Psychology](/category/consumer-psychology/)** — the science of why people buy, remember, and recommend. Dual-process theory. Behavioral economics. Mental accounting. Translated into practitioner frameworks you can actually use.

**[Research Methods](/category/research-methods/)** — how to design research, interpret data, and avoid the methodological errors that make most "data-driven" marketing decisions embarrassingly weak.

**[Content Strategy](/category/content-strategy/)** — frameworks for building content that compounds. Built on search science, audience psychology, and distribution logic — not "post consistently" advice.

## What this site is not

- **Not** a thought leadership play dressed as education
- **Not** sponsored or monetized by tools I'm recommending
- **Not** designed to generate impressions by confirming what you already believe
