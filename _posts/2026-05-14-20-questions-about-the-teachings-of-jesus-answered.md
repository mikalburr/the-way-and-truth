These are the questions people actually ask when they start reading the Gospels seriously. Each answer is grounded in what Jesus himself said — with a flag when the question itself assumes a tradition rather than something Jesus taught.

---

**1. What did Jesus say was the most important thing?**

Matthew 22:37-40: *"Love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind... And the second is like it: Love your neighbor as yourself. All the Law and the Prophets hang on these two commandments."* He was asked for the greatest commandment and gave two inseparable ones. Total love of God expressed through total love of neighbor. Everything else hangs from this.

---

**2. What did Jesus say about how to be saved?**

The question itself assumes a specific framing Jesus did not always use. His language was more often about entering the Kingdom, about eternal life, about following him. The closest he comes to the modern "salvation" framing: *"Repent and believe the good news"* (Mark 1:15). The *metanoia* (complete reorientation) and *pistis* (trust and allegiance) are both required. Not one without the other.

---

**3. Did Jesus teach that faith alone saves?**

This is primarily Paul's framing, not Jesus's. Jesus consistently connected salvation/Kingdom entry to both faith and action. Matthew 7:21: *"Not everyone who says to me, 'Lord, Lord,' will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only the one who does the will of my Father."* John 3:36: *"Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life, but whoever rejects the Son will not see life."* Jesus used both belief language and obedience language without systematically separating them.

---

**4. What did Jesus teach about hell?**

He taught its reality and used vivid imagery for it. *Gehenna* (translated "hell") refers to the Valley of Hinnom outside Jerusalem — a place of perpetual burning. He warned of it more often than he described heaven. Matthew 10:28: *"Be afraid of the One who can destroy both soul and body in hell."* Matthew 25:46: *"Then they will go away to eternal punishment, but the righteous to eternal life."* He was not vague about eternal consequence. He used it primarily as a warning against self-deception and religious hypocrisy, not primarily as an evangelistic tool against unbelievers.

---

**5. What did Jesus say about prayer?**

Pray in secret, not for performance (Matthew 6:5-6). Use the Lord's Prayer as a structural model, not a repeated script (Matthew 6:9-13). Be persistent (Luke 18:1-8). Pray in alignment with his will (John 15:7: *"If you remain in me and my words remain in you, ask whatever you wish"*). Ask, seek, knock — the Father gives good gifts (Matthew 7:7-11). He never taught the "name it and claim it" formula. He taught relational, dependent, honest communication with the Father.

---

**6. What did Jesus say about money and wealth?**

More than almost any other topic. The summary: you cannot serve God and money simultaneously (Matthew 6:24). Where your treasure is, your heart follows (Matthew 6:21). Store up Kingdom treasure, not earthly treasure (Matthew 6:19-20). Seek the Kingdom first and provision follows (Matthew 6:33). The rich young ruler was asked to sell everything (Mark 10:21). Wealth competes with Kingdom entry (Mark 10:23-25). He was not anti-wealth in principle; he was anti-wealth-as-master.

---

**7. What did Jesus say about loving enemies?**

Matthew 5:44: *"Love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you."* This is not softened elsewhere. In Luke 6:27-28 he adds: *"Do good to those who hate you, bless those who curse you, pray for those who mistreat you."* The love commanded is *agape* — chosen goodwill, not sentiment. The reason: *"that you may be children of your Father in heaven"* (Matthew 5:45). Kingdom citizens behave like the King. This is the most countercultural and most demanding ethical teaching he gave.

---

**8. What did Jesus teach about suffering?**

He normalized it for Kingdom citizens. *"In this world you will have trouble"* (John 16:33). *"If they persecuted me, they will persecute you also"* (John 15:20). The call to discipleship includes taking up the cross (Matthew 16:24). The Beatitudes explicitly bless those who mourn, who are meek, who are persecuted (Matthew 5:3-12). He did not promise suffering-free following. He promised his presence and ultimate vindication: *"But take heart! I have overcome the world"* (John 16:33).

---

**9. What did Jesus say about marriage?**

Matthew 19:4-6: *"Haven't you read that at the beginning the Creator 'made them male and female,' and said, 'For this reason a man will leave his father and mother and be united to his wife, and the two will become one flesh'? So they are no longer two, but one flesh. Therefore what God has joined together, let no one separate."* He grounded marriage in Genesis creation — one man, one woman, one flesh union — and declared its permanence. On divorce, he allowed it only for sexual immorality (Matthew 19:9), citing the hardness of human hearts as the reason Moses permitted it, not the divine design.

---

**10. What did Jesus teach about the Kingdom of God?**

It is the central subject of his teaching. It is a present reality that has come near (Mark 1:15). It belongs to the poor in spirit, the persecuted (Matthew 5:3, 10). It grows from small beginnings (mustard seed, leaven — Matthew 13:31-33). It demands total priority (Matthew 13:44-46). It is entered through new birth (John 3:3-5). It is not of this world in the sense that it does not operate by the world's power structures (John 18:36). It is both present (Luke 17:21) and coming in its fullness (Matthew 6:10).

---

**11. What did Jesus say about the cross?**

He predicted it three times in the Synoptics with increasing specificity (Mark 8:31, 9:31, 10:33-34). He described it as a ransom: *"The Son of Man did not come to be served, but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many"* (Mark 10:45). He called his disciples to their own cross: *"Whoever wants to be my disciple must deny themselves and take up their cross and follow me"* (Matthew 16:24). The cross is simultaneously the means of redemption and the pattern of Kingdom life — the path through death to life.

---

**12. What did Jesus teach about eternal life?**

John 17:3 is his clearest definition: *"Now this is eternal life: that they know you, the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom you have sent."* It is relational knowledge — *ginosko* — not duration. It is available in the present: *"Whoever hears my word and believes him who sent me has eternal life and has crossed over from death to life"* (John 5:24). Present tense. It includes a future resurrection dimension (John 5:28-29) but is not only about what happens after death.

---

**13. What did Jesus say about the Holy Spirit?**

Extensively in the farewell discourse (John 14-16). The Spirit is the *Paraclete* — the one called alongside to advocate, help, and counsel (John 14:16-17). He will guide believers into all truth (John 16:13). He will convict the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment (John 16:8). He will glorify Jesus (John 16:14). He will teach the disciples everything and remind them of everything Jesus said (John 14:26). He is sent by the Father in Jesus's name and comes when Jesus departs (John 16:7).

---

**14. What did Jesus say about the church?**

The word *ekklesia* (translated "church") appears only twice in the Gospels — both in Matthew (Matthew 16:18 and 18:17). In Matthew 16:18, Jesus says: *"On this rock I will build my church, and the gates of Hades will not overcome it."* In Matthew 18:17, he describes the community of disciples as the court of accountability for interpersonal sin. Jesus's primary language was not "church" but Kingdom community — disciples gathered around him and his teaching.

---

**15. What did Jesus teach about fasting?**

That it should be done for God, not for visibility. Matthew 6:16-18: *"When you fast, do not look somber as the hypocrites do... But when you fast, put oil on your head and wash your face, so that it will not be obvious to others that you are fasting, but only to your Father, who is unseen."* He assumed his disciples would fast — "when you fast," not "if." He also distinguished between the old fasting of mourning (appropriate before his arrival) and the new reality of his presence (Matthew 9:14-15).

---

**16. What did Jesus say about anger?**

He escalated the command against murder to include anger and contempt. Matthew 5:21-22: *"Anyone who is angry with a brother or sister will be subject to judgment. Again, anyone who says to a brother or sister, 'Raca,' is answerable to the court. And anyone who says, 'You fool!' will be in danger of the fire of hell."* He also demonstrated anger himself — in the Temple (John 2:14-17) and with the Pharisees' hardness of heart (Mark 3:5). The issue is contemptuous anger that treats people as less than human, not all anger.

---

**17. What did Jesus teach about judging others?**

Matthew 7:1-5: Do not judge hypocritically — remove the plank from your own eye first. Then you will see clearly to help your brother. John 7:24: *"Stop judging by mere appearances, but instead judge correctly."* He is not prohibiting discernment or evaluation. He is prohibiting self-deception in judgment — using others' failures to avoid seeing your own, and judging by surface without seeing substance. Mature Kingdom discernment is both possible and required.

---

**18. What did Jesus say about worry?**

Matthew 6:25: *"Do not worry about your life."* The Greek *merimnaō* — to be divided, to be pulled in multiple directions. The antidote is not positive thinking but knowledge of the Father: *"Your heavenly Father knows what you need"* (Matthew 6:32). And Kingdom priority: *"Seek first his kingdom... and all these things will be given to you as well"* (Matthew 6:33). Anxiety is not primarily an emotional problem. It is a faith problem — the operating system of someone who has not yet brought their trust fully into alignment with who the Father is.

---

**19. What did Jesus say about truth?**

John 8:32: *"Then you will know the truth, and the truth will set you free."* In context, this truth is not abstract — it is the teaching of Jesus, known through remaining in it. John 14:6: *"I am the way and the truth and the life."* He does not say he teaches the truth or points to the truth. He is the truth — the reality in whom all things hold together. John 18:37: *"Everyone on the side of truth listens to me."*

---

**20. What did Jesus say about following him?**

That it costs everything and is worth it. Mark 1:17: *"Come, follow me."* Matthew 16:24: *"Whoever wants to be my disciple must deny themselves and take up their cross and follow me."* Matthew 13:44-46: The Kingdom is worth selling everything to obtain. Luke 14:33: *"Those of you who do not give up everything you have cannot be my disciples."* And the other side: *"I have come that they may have life, and have it to the full"* (John 10:10). The cost is total. The life on the other side is total. Both are true simultaneously.
