This is not a quiz to make you feel good about how far you have come.

It is a diagnostic tool — 25 honest questions across five domains that will show you the actual shape of your warrior development. Not your aspirations. Not your best days. The actual shape of your life as it stands.

Answer each question honestly. The score does not matter. The pattern matters — because the pattern tells you where your real work is.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

Rate each question on a 1-5 scale:
- **1** — This is rarely or never true of me
- **3** — This is sometimes true, inconsistently
- **5** — This is consistently true, even under pressure

The benchmark for each question is not perfection. It is consistent, habitual reality. If you would have to have an extraordinary week to answer 5, it is not a 5.

Total your scores at the end of each domain. Note where you scored lowest. That is your primary work area.

---

## Domain 1: Identity (Questions 1-5)

**Q1. I know who I am in Christ at a level that holds under pressure — not just as theological belief but as internal reality.**

*Benchmark at 5:* When you are criticized, publicly embarrassed, or fail at something significant, your first internal response is grounded in your identity as a son of God — not in performance recovery or reputation management.

*What a low score means:* Your sense of self is still primarily performance-based. Every challenge is a potential threat to who you are rather than a test of what you can do.

---

**Q2. I know the difference between my identity in Christ and my current behavioral level of development.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You can hold "I am a son of God" and "I am still growing in this area" simultaneously — without the second undermining the first.

*What a low score means:* You either conflate your identity with your performance (you are good when you do well, bad when you fail) or you use grace language to avoid accountability.

---

**Q3. I regularly speak to myself in the language of who God says I am — not in the language of my failures or the opinions of others.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You catch and correct the internal narration when it slips into shame, comparison, or defeat. You replace it actively with Scripture-based identity language.

*What a low score means:* Your internal narration is primarily shaped by performance history, others' opinions, or ambient cultural messages about what a man should be.

---

**Q4. My identity does not depend on my role, my income, my appearance, or my family's regard for me.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You know who you are when the role is stripped away — when the job ends, when your family is disappointed in you, when you are alone and no one is watching.

*What a low score means:* You are one significant loss away from an identity crisis.

---

**Q5. I can receive correction, criticism, and rebuke without becoming defensive or collapsing.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You hear criticism as information about your behavior or blind spots — not as a verdict on your worth. You can say "you are right" without it costing you your sense of self.

*What a low score means:* Criticism is still a threat to your identity, which means you are either avoiding it (passive) or defending against it aggressively (reactive).

## Domain 2: Discipline (Questions 6-11)

**Q6. I engage with Scripture daily — not as a check-box but as the primary source for understanding my assignment.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You have a consistent daily practice that has not broken down in more than three consecutive days in the last three months.

---

**Q7. My prayer life is a real communication channel with God — not a crisis-only resource or a performance ritual.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You pray when nothing is wrong. You pray before decisions, not just after disasters. God's voice is a regular input into your daily orientation.

---

**Q8. I govern my physical body — training consistently, eating with intentionality, and sleeping with discipline.**

*Benchmark at 5:* Your body is maintained as a tool of mission, not indulged as a source of comfort. Paul's "strict training" (1 Corinthians 9:25) describes your approach.

---

**Q9. I fast with intentionality — not as a diet but as a discipline of governing appetite and seeking clarity.**

*Benchmark at 5:* Fasting is a regular practice in your life. You know from experience what it does to your clarity, your prayer, and your relationship to desire.

---

**Q10. I govern my use of screens, entertainment, and passive consumption.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You choose what you consume rather than consuming by default. Your entertainment does not exceed your investment in things that actually develop you.

---

**Q11. My disciplines hold when I do not feel like maintaining them.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You distinguish between motivation and discipline. When motivation is absent, you act from discipline. The consistency of your practice is higher than the consistency of your enthusiasm.

## Domain 3: Brotherhood (Questions 12-15)

**Q12. I have at least one man in my life who knows the real state of my interior — my actual struggles, not a curated version.**

*Benchmark at 5:* There is a man who has heard you say "I am losing ground in this area" and who has stayed in it with you. Not just a friend — a brother in the actual sense.

---

**Q13. I am sharpening the men around me, not just drawing from them.**

*Benchmark at 5:* The men in your life are growing because of your presence. You ask the hard questions. You call them forward. Iron sharpens iron (Proverbs 27:17) is a two-way description.

---

**Q14. I am pursuing brotherhood intentionally — not waiting for it to happen.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You have initiated real conversations about spiritual and personal development with at least one man in the last month. Brotherhood does not find you; you build it.

---

**Q15. My primary social investment is in relationships that produce growth, not just comfort.**

*Benchmark at 5:* The men you spend the most time with are men who make you better — not just men who make you comfortable.

## Domain 4: Leadership (Questions 16-20)

**Q16. I am actively governing the primary domain God has assigned me — family, work, or community.**

*Benchmark at 5:* People in your primary domain know who is leading — because you are visibly, consistently present and directing the culture of that domain.

---

**Q17. I initiate the hard conversations in my relationships rather than avoiding them.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You do not wait for problems to become crises before addressing them. You bring the difficult truth when it is still possible to address it constructively.

---

**Q18. I lead from service, not from a need for recognition.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You can identify recent acts of leadership that no one noticed or credited you for — and that you did not need them to.

---

**Q19. I am mentoring or investing in someone younger or less developed than myself.**

*Benchmark at 5:* Paul invested in Timothy. Every Kingdom man is called to reproduce what he is building. You are doing this with someone specific, consistently.

---

**Q20. My leadership is visible to my family or close community — they would describe me as a leader.**

*Benchmark at 5:* Ask your wife or a close friend: "Would you describe me as a leader in our home/community?" The honest answer to that question reveals the gap between how you see yourself and how your leadership actually lands.

## Domain 5: Warfare (Questions 21-25)

**Q21. I understand the nature of spiritual warfare well enough to identify specific attacks in my specific life.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You can name the primary area of spiritual attack in your life right now — the specific lie, the specific temptation, the specific pattern — and you are engaging it with specific prayer and Scripture.

---

**Q22. I put on the armor of God (Ephesians 6:13-18) as a daily practice, not a crisis measure.**

*Benchmark at 5:* The armor is part of your daily orientation — not something you reach for when you are in trouble, but something you put on before the trouble arrives.

---

**Q23. I recognize when I am fighting flesh-level battles instead of engaging the real spiritual war.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You catch yourself when you are consuming energy on arguments, offenses, or interpersonal conflicts that are secondary — and you redirect to the primary spiritual work.

---

**Q24. I pray with authority — as a man who has been given real Kingdom authority — not just with requests.**

*Benchmark at 5:* You know the difference between intercession and petition, between declaring and asking. You pray from a posture of authority under God, not from a posture of hoping he might respond.

---

**Q25. Suffering and setback deepen my engagement with God rather than producing retreat.**

*Benchmark at 5:* Your track record shows that hard seasons drive you toward prayer, Scripture, and brotherhood — not away from them. You have evidence that this is actually true of you.

## Reading Your Results

Add your totals:
- **Identity:** ___ / 25
- **Discipline:** ___ / 30
- **Brotherhood:** ___ / 20
- **Leadership:** ___ / 25
- **Warfare:** ___ / 25
- **Grand Total:** ___ / 125

**What to do with the numbers:**

Do not average them. Find the domain where you scored the lowest. That is your primary development area. Warrior growth is not about raising all scores simultaneously — it is about identifying your weakest position and reinforcing it.

A chain breaks at its weakest link. A warrior falls at his least-governed domain.

Pick the lowest-scoring domain. Go back and read the questions. Identify the single lowest-scoring question in that domain. Make that one question your assignment for the next 30 days.

Not all 25. One.

That is how warriors are built — not by trying to do everything at once, but by identifying the specific ground that needs to be taken and taking it, systematically, over time.

The checklist will be here next month. Run it again. Track the movement. That movement, compounded over years, is what transformation actually looks like.
