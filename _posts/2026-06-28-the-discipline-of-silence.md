"In the morning, while it was still very dark, he got up and went out to a deserted place, and there he prayed." — Mark 1:35

The deserted place is not incidental. Jesus did not pray in the marketplace. He did not pray with music in the background. He did not pray with notifications buzzing. He went somewhere there was nothing, and there he was with the Father.

Modern men have built lives in which deserted places do not exist. The phone is in the pocket, the podcast is in the earbuds, the TV is on in the background. The silence has been engineered out of daily life — and most men do not notice, because they were the ones who engineered it out. The noise is the anesthetic. It is what makes the inside tolerable.

The discipline of silence is the deliberate dismantling of that anesthetic. It is harder than fasting for most men. It is also more revealing.

## What the Noise Is For

Be honest about what the constant audio input is doing. It is not making you smarter. It is regulating your nervous system. The podcast on the commute is keeping the mind from wandering into the things you have been avoiding. The music while you work is suppressing the low-grade anxiety that would otherwise surface. The phone in the bathroom is preventing you from being alone with yourself for even three minutes.

The noise is an emotional regulation strategy. It works in the short term. It costs everything in the long term — because the things you are using noise to suppress do not go away. They accumulate. They turn into the irritability, the restlessness, the inability to sleep, the marriage problems, the slow drift away from God that you cannot quite name.

Silence forces the suppressed material to the surface. That is its function. That is also why most men cannot sit in it long enough to receive the benefit.

## The Biblical Pattern

Scripture is dense with silence as a discipline.

Elijah on Horeb, in 1 Kings 19. He has just won the contest on Mount Carmel. He is now exhausted, suicidal, hiding in a cave. God shows up — but not in the wind, not in the earthquake, not in the fire. God shows up in "a sound of sheer silence" (1 Kings 19:12). The man who has been deafened by his own crisis can only hear God in silence.

Psalm 46:10 — "Be still, and know that I am God." The Hebrew is *raphah* — cease, let drop, relax. Stop striving. Stop noising. The knowing follows the stilling.

Lamentations 3:26 — "It is good that one should wait quietly for the salvation of the Lord." Quietness is not absence of activity. It is the posture of waiting, of receptivity, of trust.

Jesus withdrew constantly. Luke 5:16 — "He would withdraw to deserted places and pray." Not occasionally. Not when overwhelmed. As a pattern. The man with the most demanding ministry in human history protected silence as a non-negotiable.

## How to Start

The entry is harder than it sounds. The first attempts will feel like failure. They are not. They are the beginning.

**Five minutes. Same chair. No phone.** Set a timer. Sit. Do not pray out loud. Do not read. Do not plan. Just be. The mind will protest immediately. It will throw up tasks, anxieties, random memories, urgent texts you suddenly remember you need to send. Let them surface. Do not chase them. Do not act on them. Sit.

**Notice the first urge to reach for input.** It will usually arrive within 90 seconds. The hand will move toward the pocket. The thought will appear that this is a waste of time and you should be doing something. Notice it. Do not yield. The discomfort is the work.

**Extend to twenty minutes.** Add two minutes a week. By the end of a month, you are at twenty. This is the threshold where something changes — the mental noise has cycled through enough times that it begins to settle. Below the noise is what you have not heard in years.

**Pair it with the morning anchor or evening close.** Silence is not separate from the rest of the spiritual life. Attach it to an existing rhythm — the [morning anchor routine](/2026/05/31/building-a-morning-anchor-routine/), the end of the workday, the last thing before sleep. The pairing makes it durable.

## What Surfaces

When the noise drops away, several things become available.

**The inner state.** You start to know what you actually feel. Not what you should feel. Not what would be acceptable to feel. What is actually there. For many men, this has been buried under input for so long that the first weeks of silence are disorienting. The data is unfamiliar.

**Conviction.** Things you have been doing that are wrong, things you have been avoiding that are right — these come into view in silence because there is no noise to drown them out. The Holy Spirit's conviction is usually quiet. It cannot get through a podcast. It can get through silence.

**God's voice.** Not audibly. Not dramatically. But the impression, the sense, the leading — these communicate in silence. Men who have never heard God in their lives often start hearing him in the second month of consistent silent practice. He has been speaking. They have been listening to other things.

**Direction.** The decisions you have been agonizing over often clarify in silence. Not because new information arrives — but because the noise that was preventing you from seeing what you already knew goes away. The answer was already there. You could not hear it.

## The Warrior Connection

A man who cannot tolerate silence cannot be trusted with weight. He will medicate every difficulty with input. He will not develop the inner resources that hard times require. He will be the man whose inner life is shallow because he has never been still enough to deepen it.

The [Christian warrior mindset](/2026/05/12/christian-warrior-mindset/) requires interior strength. Interior strength is built in interior space. The man who has never been in his own interior cannot defend it, deepen it, or operate from it. Silence is how that space gets cultivated.

Silence is also where prayer becomes real. Not the request-list prayer of distraction. The communion prayer of a son with a Father. That kind of prayer requires the conditions that silence creates.

## The Practice for Tomorrow

Tonight, decide on the time. Set the chair. Put the phone in another room. In the morning, sit for five minutes. Do not check anything when you finish. Just stand up and go into the day.

Do it again the next morning. And the next. The discomfort is real. Stay in it. The noise has been your master long enough. It is time to find out what you sound like when there is nothing else playing.
