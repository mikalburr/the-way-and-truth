A journaling Bible is a serious study tool if you use it as one. It is a decorative project if you treat it as one. The difference is not the Bible. It is what you do with it.

The premise is simple. Most readers underline in their Bible, write a date in the margin, and then forget what they were thinking. A journaling Bible gives you enough space to actually capture observations, cross-references, prayers, and applications next to the text where you saw them. Done over years, the Bible itself becomes a record of how God has been speaking to you.

These five are the best journaling Bibles in print. I have used three of them in regular study and have evaluated the other two carefully. Here they are.

---

## 1. ESV Single Column Journaling Bible

  asin="1433533766"

  verdict="The best all-in-one journaling Bible. Single column, generous margin, durable construction. If you can only own one journaling Bible, get this one."

**What it covers:** The full ESV Bible in single-column format with a 2-inch ruled margin running the length of every page. The single-column layout means longer text lines and an easier reading flow than double-column Bibles. The ruled margin is wide enough for substantive notes — 4-6 lines of writing per verse if you write small.

**Who it's for:** Men who want one journaling Bible they will carry to church, use in personal study, and write in for years. The construction is durable, the paper is thick enough to handle most pens, and the format is genuinely usable for both reading and writing.

**What it adds:** Balance. Other journaling Bibles trade off — more margin space at the cost of weight, or thinner paper at the cost of bleed-through. The ESV Single Column manages to give you usable margin without making the Bible unwieldy.

**One honest note:** 2 inches of margin is generous for verse-level notes but tight for paragraph-length reflections. If you write long, you will run out of room. For longer-form journaling, supplement this with the Scripture Journal series below or with a separate notebook.

## 2. NIV Journal the Word Bible

{% include book-card.html
  title="NIV Journal the Word Bible"
  author="Zondervan"
  asin="0310445795"
  description="Double-column NIV text with wide single-column ruled margins on every page. The most readable journaling Bible for men who want the NIV translation and full margin space."
  verdict="The best journaling Bible in the NIV translation. Double-column layout with full single-column margins gives you both readability and writing room."

**What it covers:** The full 2011 NIV text in double-column format with a wide single-column lined margin running on the outer edge of each page. The margin is approximately the same width as the ESV Single Column Journaling Bible, but the double-column text layout means more verses per page.

**Who it's for:** Men who prefer the NIV translation and want a journaling Bible that does not require switching translations to journal in. The NIV is the most widely read English translation in active use, and the Journal the Word edition is the best journaling implementation in that translation.

**What it adds:** Translation choice. Bible-buying decisions are heavily driven by translation preference, and most premium journaling Bibles default to the ESV. The NIV Journal the Word fills that gap competently.

**One honest note:** The paper is thinner than the ESV Single Column Journaling Bible and ghosting is more visible. Pigma Microns or fine-point gel pens minimize the issue. Avoid heavy fountain pens.

**What it covers:** The Scripture Journal series is a paperback series with one volume per book of the New Testament (19 volumes total for the NT set; complete OT and NT sets also available). Each volume is approximately the size of a thin paperback. The right-hand page contains the ESV text in single-column format. The left-hand page is fully blank, lined, and ready for notes.

**Who it's for:** Men who do serious book-by-book study and want unlimited margin space without a heavy all-in-one Bible. If you are spending six months in Romans, you carry the Romans volume. If you are teaching a series on John, you mark up the John volume.

**What it adds:** Space. Every other journaling Bible compromises between text and margin. The Scripture Journal eliminates the compromise. You have a full lined page per spread for whatever notes, outlines, prayers, or cross-references you want.

**One honest note:** Carrying 19 volumes (or 35+ for the full Bible) is not practical. Use this format for the books you are currently studying deeply, and use a full journaling Bible for general reading. Many serious students use both.

**What it adds:** A serious journaling option in the CSB. If you have decided on the CSB as your primary translation, this is the journaling Bible to buy.

**One honest note:** The CSB is a newer translation with less commentary and study material available than the ESV or NIV. If you regularly cross-reference your Bible reading with study resources, factor in what is available in your translation.

## How to Choose Your Journaling Bible

For most men, the decision tree is straightforward:

**If you want one journaling Bible:** ESV Single Column Journaling Bible. Best balance of margin, readability, and construction.

**If you want the NIV translation:** NIV Journal the Word Bible. Same approach in the NIV.

**If you study one book at a time deeply:** ESV Scripture Journal series. Unlimited writing space per book.

**If you prefer the CSB:** CSB Notetaking Bible. Best CSB journaling option.

**If the visual dimension matters:** NKJV Beautiful Word Bible Journal Edition.

A journaling Bible does not produce a deeper devotional life by itself. It produces one if you actually write in it — observations of the text, questions, cross-references, prayers, dates of significance. Done over a decade, the Bible itself becomes a record of how God has been working in your life. That is the actual value.

Pick one. Start writing. Date your entries.
